THE OFFICIAL REPORT OF THE FUKUSHIMA NUCLEAR ACCIDENT INDEPENDENT INVESTIGATION 
COMMISSION (NAIIC) — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY KEY FINDINGS
Published: July 2012 | National Diet of Japan
================================================================================

## CORE CONCLUSION

The report states clearly:

"This accident cannot be regarded as a natural disaster. It was a profoundly man-made 
disaster — one that could and should have been foreseen and prevented."

Although the March 11, 2011 earthquake and tsunami served as the trigger, the Fukushima
nuclear disaster was fundamentally a human-made catastrophe.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## ROOT CAUSES ANALYSIS

The report reveals multiple errors and deliberate negligence:

| Problem Area          | Specific Issue                                              |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Engineering Failures  | The plant could not withstand the March 11 events           |
| Regulatory Failure    | TEPCO, regulators, and government responded inadequately    |
| Cultural Factors      | Deep cultural traditions fostered a climate of negligence   |

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## CULTURAL ROOTS ("Made in Japan")

The report specifically identifies several deep cultural problems in Japan:

- Blind obedience to authority
- Lack of a questioning spirit
- Rigid "follow procedures" mentality
- Prioritization of group consensus above individual responsibility
- Insularity and resistance to outside oversight

The report acknowledges:
"If the people who were in the positions of responsibility had been other Japanese, 
the result would likely have been the same."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## NUCLEAR POLICY HISTORICAL CONTEXT

- After the 1970s oil crisis, Japan accelerated nuclear development for energy security
- Nuclear power was embraced by government and business as a policy goal
- "Nuclear power became an unstoppable force, immune to scrutiny by civil society"
  — Kiyoshi Kurokawa, Commission Chairman
- Regulation and promotion of nuclear power resided in the SAME government ministry,
  creating a fundamental conflict of interest

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## SYSTEMIC DEFICIENCIES

1. Information Isolation: Japan's nuclear industry failed to absorb lessons from 
   Three Mile Island and Chernobyl
2. Culture of Cover-Up: Small-scale accidents were concealed; regulatory pressure 
   was routinely resisted
3. Bureaucratic Self-Preservation: Officials' primary duty was to protect their 
   organization, not public safety

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## KEY SPECIFIC FINDINGS (from Executive Summary)

On ignoring tsunami risk (2006):
"Since 2006, the regulators and TEPCO were aware of the risk that a total outage 
of electricity at the Fukushima Daiichi plant might occur if the tsunami were to 
reach the level of the site."

On inadequate evacuation plan:
"Only 20% of the residents of the town hosting the plant knew about the accident 
when evacuation from the 3-km zone was ordered at 9:23 p.m. on March 11."

On radiation information failure:
"[The government] failed to explain the risks of radiation exposure to different 
segments of the population, such as infants and youths, expecting mothers, or 
people particularly susceptible to the risks of radiation."

On insularity from international knowledge:
"The regulators also had a negative attitude toward the importation of new advances 
in knowledge and technology from overseas. If [NISA] had passed on to TEPCO measures 
included in the B.5.b subsection of the U.S. security order that followed the 9/11 
terrorist action, and if TEPCO had put the measures in place, the accident may have 
been preventable."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## SIGNIFICANCE OF THE INVESTIGATION

"This report criticizes many individuals and organizations, but its purpose is not —
and should not be — to assign blame. The purpose must be to learn lessons from 
this disaster."

The NAIIC was the first investigation body in Japanese history authorized by the 
legislature to operate independently of the bureaucratic system.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## REPORT CORE MESSAGE

The fundamental cause of this disaster was institutional and cultural — nuclear 
policy was monopolized by the political-business elite, regulation failed, and 
the spirit of critical inquiry was absent. The report calls on Japanese citizens 
to reflect deeply on their responsibilities as individuals in a democratic society.

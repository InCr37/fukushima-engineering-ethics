## VIDEO DESCRIPTION

This video report was produced for the 4th anniversary of the Fukushima disaster 
(March 11, 2015), covering:

### Background Events
- On March 11, 2011, Japan's most powerful earthquake triggered a massive tsunami
- More than 18,000 people died along Japan's northeastern coast
- The disaster triggered the meltdown of three reactors at Fukushima Daiichi 
  Nuclear Power Station

### Ongoing Cleanup
- The cleanup operation is expected to take FORTY YEARS
- Estimated cost: TENS OF BILLIONS OF DOLLARS

### Reporting Focus
The video interviewed two categories of key subjects:

1. KEY FIGURES from critical moments of the Fukushima crisis
   - These include plant operators, government officials, and emergency responders
   who were on the ground during the disaster

2. DISPLACED RESIDENTS — Tens of thousands of people forced to evacuate from 
   contaminated communities who continue to live in "nuclear limbo"
   - They remain unable to return to their homes
   - Their futures remain uncertain years after the disaster

## KEY QUOTE (Title)
"Nuclear power and humans cannot coexist" — sentiment expressed by affected residents

## CONTEXT NOTES
- This Guardian video was part of broader coverage marking the 4-year anniversary
- The phrase "nuclear limbo" refers to evacuees still unable to return to areas 
  within the evacuation zone near the plant
- At the time of filming (2015), the cleanup was still in very early stages

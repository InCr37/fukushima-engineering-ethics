# Determine

## — What Went Wrong? —

### Abstract

The Fukushima disaster was not caused by an unforeseeable tsunami.  
It was a manmade catastrophe, the result of systemic failures at multiple levels.

***

## 1. Faster, Better, Cheaper vs Safety

### Key Question

Why were no countermeasures made to prevent tsunami damage?

### Issues Identified

*   TEPCO operated under a **money‑first business logic**:  
    safety regulations would interfere with operations and reduce profits.
*   When experts proposed tsunami countermeasures, **TEPCO rejected them** as:
    *   “Too expensive”
    *   “Impractical”
*   This happened **despite the risk of a catastrophic tsunami being evident as early as 2006** (five years before the disaster).
*   **TEPCO is the company that owns the nuclear plant.**

***

## Engineering Ethics Principle

**Engineers shall reject practices that harm public welfare.**

### Conclusion

**Safety cannot be traded away for cost.**

***

## 2. Monitoring Department Collusion

### Organizational Roles and Problems

| Organization | Role                   | Problem                          |
| ------------ | ---------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **METI**     | Promotes nuclear power | Made NISA part of their ministry |
| **NISA**     | Nuclear regulator      | Not independent, under METI      |
| **TEPCO**    | Plant operator         | Resisting regulations, hid risks |

### Key Question

Why was a situation where everything failed allowed to occur?

### Issues Identified

*   Government regulators had **benefit relationships** with the nuclear plant company.
*   There was **no effective monitoring and supervision** to keep the plant safe.
*   Engineers who raised warnings were:
    *   Overruled
    *   Ignored
    *   Punished
*   **TEPCO is the company that owns the nuclear plant.**

***

## Engineering Ethics Principle

**Engineers must advise consequences when their judgment is overruled by non‑technical authority.**

### Conclusion

**Collusion caused regulators to fail at protecting the nation from nuclear accidents — which was their duty.**

***

## 3. Engineers Must Ensure Recovery, Not Just Prevention

### Core Idea

Engineers not only need to ensure systems **do not fail**, but also ensure they **can be fixed quickly when they do fail**.

### Key Question

Why was the nuclear plant unprepared when the tsunami came?

### Issues Identified

*   No emergency training
*   No evacuation plan
*   No preparation for scenarios where the plant could explode
*   Prevention alone is not enough

> Like a house that prevents fires **but also conducts fire drills**, preparation must include failure response.

***

## Engineering Ethics Principle

**Responsible engineers don’t just prevent failure; they prepare for failure and its consequences.**

### Conclusion – Questions Engineers Must Ask

*   What happens when multiple systems fail simultaneously?
*   How do we limit damage?
*   How do we recover quickly?

# DETERMINE — What went Wrong?
## Abstract

> *The Fukushima disaster was not caused by an unforeseeable tsunami. It was a **manmade catastrophe** — the result of systemic failures at multiple levels.*

---

## Point 1: Faster, Better, Cheaper vs Safety

**Why no countermeasures were made to prevent tsunami**

- TEPCO(the company who own the nuclear plant) operated under a **money first** business logic: safety regulations would **interfere with operations and reduce profits**
- Experts proposed tsunami countermeasures → TEPCO said: "too expensive", "impractical", even though the risk is evident

**Which Principle it reflects:** Engineers shall reject practices that harm public welfare.

**Conclusion:** Safety cannot be traded away for cost.

---

## Point 2: Multi-Departmental Monitoring Failed / Collusion

**The collusion between the government, the regulators and TEPCO allowed things to go wrong as a whole** because the regulators have benefit relationship with the nuclear plant company

| Organization | Role | Problem |
|------------|------|---------|
| METI | Promotes nuclear power | Made NISA part of their ministry |
| NISA | Nuclear regulator | Not independent — captured by METI |
| TEPCO | Plant operator | Resisted regulations, hid risks |

**Results:**
→ Engineers who warned were overruled, ignored, or punished.
→ The whistleblower at Millstone was fired and blacklisted.
→ Not only by the plant, but also by regulator officials.

**Which Principle it reflects:**  Engineers must advise consequences when their judgment is overruled by non-technical authority.

**Conclusion:** Collusion make regulators fails their duty: make the nation safe from nuclear accidents."

---

## Point 3: Engineers not just need to ensure it don't go wrong, but also ensure it could be fixed quickly when it go wrong

**Why when tsunami came, the nuclear plant was unprepared:**

- The plant have No emergency training. The government have no evacuation plan.
- The former head of TEPCO said: *"the risk of a tsunami never crossed his mind"* 
- They do not think about what happend when the plant is going to blew up. Result in:
- Evacuation chaotic. Communication delayed.
- Evacuation zone kept changing over a year. Some residents evacuated to higher radiation areas.
- ~70% of nearest residents evacuated 4+ times in one year.

**This is arrogant engineering and this is not good,** Engineers must assume crisis can hit any second and prepare for it.

**Conclusion:** 

**What Question you also need to think:**
- What happens when multiple systems fail simultaneously?
- How do we limit damage?
- How do we recover quickly?

Like a house that prevents fire but also does fire drills — prevention alone is not enough.

---

## Conclusion in one slide

| Failure | Evidence | Ethical Principle Violated |
|---------|----------|---------------------------|
| **1. Cost over safety** | TEPCO postponed countermeasures; "too expensive" | Public welfare over profits |
| **2. Regulatory collusion** | NISA inside METI; warnings silenced; | Ethical Regulation |
| **3. No failure planning** | Evacuation chaos; 4+ evacuations; arrogant to tsunami risk | Accountability for consequences |

> **These are not individual errors — they are systemic ethical problems.**

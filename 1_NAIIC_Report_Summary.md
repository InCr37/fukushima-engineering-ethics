# NAIIC Report Key Findings
## Fukushima Nuclear Accident Independent Investigation Commission

---

## Key Facts for "Describe"

### The Accident (March 11, 2011)
- **Magnitude 9.0 earthquake + 15m tsunami** struck Japan's east coast
- Fukushima Daiichi had **6 reactors** (Units 1-3 operating, Units 4-6 under inspection)
- All operating reactors automatically shut down (SCRAM) upon earthquake
- **Total station blackout** — both external power lines failed, diesel generators flooded
- **Units 1, 3, 4 experienced hydrogen explosions**
- **Unit 2 containment breached**
- Level 7 (Severe Accident) on INES scale

### Timeline
| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| 14:46 | Earthquake — reactors SCRAM |
| 15:37 | Tsunami strikes (14m height) |
| ~15:38 | Station blackout begins |
| ~18:10 | Unit 1 reactor core exposure |
| 15:36 (Mar 12) | Unit 1 hydrogen explosion |
| 11:01 (Mar 14) | Unit 3 hydrogen explosion |
| 6:00 (Mar 15) | Unit 2 containment damage |

### Stakeholders
- **TEPCO** (Tokyo Electric Power Company) — plant operator
- **NISA** (Nuclear and Industrial Safety Agency) — regulator
- **METI** (Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry) — promoting nuclear power
- **Japanese Government / Kantei** (Prime Minister's Office)
- **Nuclear Safety Commission (NSC)**
- **160,000+ evacuated residents**
- **Plant workers** (including "Fukushima 50")

## Key Findings for "Determine"

### The Commission concluded it was a **"manmade disaster"**

> "The TEPCO Fukushima Nuclear Power Plant accident was the result of **collusion between the government, the regulators and TEPCO**, and the lack of governance by said parties. They effectively **betrayed the nation's right to be safe from nuclear accidents**."

### Direct Causes Were Foreseeable

1. **Tsunami risk was known since 2006**
   - NISA and TEPCO aware that total electricity outage could occur if tsunami struck
   - No regulations created, no protective steps taken

2. **No anti-seismic reinforcements implemented**
   - Unit 1's earthquake resistance set at only 265 Gal (remarkably low)
   - New guidelines in 2006 required assessment but deadline postponed to 2016
   - **Zero reinforcements added to Units 1-3 by March 2011**

3. **Design flaw**: 5 reactors had older design, more vulnerable to tsunami

### Regulatory Capture
- NISA created as part of METI (promotes nuclear power, not independent)
- TEPCO resisted new regulations through Federation of Electric Power Companies
- Regulators were "reluctant to actively create new regulations" because they'd "firmly committed themselves to the idea that nuclear power plants were safe"

### Emergency Response Failures

1. **Information chaos**: Only 20% of residents in host town knew about accident when evacuation ordered at 21:23 (March 11)

2. **Delayed communication**: Most residents within 10km learned about accident only when evacuation issued at 5:44 on March 12 — **12+ hours after Article 15 notification**

3. **Evacuation problems**:
   - ~50% of Namie residents evacuated to high radiation areas
   - Some residents evacuated 4+ times
   - 70% of residents near plant evacuated 4+ times in the year following

4. **Worker safety failures**:
   - ~30% of workers never told their cumulative radiation dose
   - Workers forced to share dosimeters
   - Many worked without consent
   - Internal radiation management insufficient

### What the Commission Found

> "We found **ignorance and arrogance** unforgivable for anyone or any organization that deals with nuclear power. We found a **disregard for global trends** and a **disregard for public safety**. We found a **habit of adherence to conditions based on conventional procedures** and prior practices, with a priority on avoiding risk to the organization. We found an **organization-driven mindset** that prioritized benefits to the organization at the expense of the public."

## Alternatives for "Discuss"

### What Could Have Been Done Differently?

1. **Before the accident:**
   - Implement tsunami countermeasures when risk became known in 2006
   - Complete anti-seismic reinforcements on schedule
   - Independent regulator not captured by industry
   - Genuine emergency preparedness drills (only 10-15% of nearby residents had any evacuation training)

2. **During the accident:**
   - Clear chain of command (Kantei disrupted planned chain by contacting plant directly)
   - Faster public communication about severity
   - Proper radiation monitoring and disclosure (SPEEDI data not released)
   - Better worker protection and consent

3. **Masao Yoshida's decision:**
   - Ignored TEPCO HQ order to stop seawater injection into Unit 1
   - This likely prevented worse catastrophe
   - Sakae Muto (VP) called it "technically appropriate"

## Recommendations for "Decide"

The Commission made **7 recommendations**:

1. **Monitoring of nuclear regulatory body by National Diet**
   - Permanent committee to supervise regulators

2. **Reform crisis management system**
   - Clear role boundaries between national/local government and operators
   - Consolidated chain of command

3. **Government responsibility for public health and welfare**
   - State-funded medical diagnosis/treatment
   - Full information disclosure with public safety as priority

4. **Reforming operators**
   - Cross-monitoring systems for safety
   - TEPCO dramatic corporate reform
   - Agency appointed by National Diet to monitor all operators

5. **Criteria for new regulatory body** — must be:
   - Independent (from government, operators, politics)
   - Transparent
   - Professional (meet global standards)
   - Consolidated (consolidate emergency functions)
   - Proactive

6. **Reforming nuclear energy laws**
   - Rewrite to meet global safety standards

7. **Earthquake damage investigation**
   - Possibility that earthquake, not just tsunami, caused damage to safety equipment

## Statistics from Survey of Evacuees (10,633 respondents)

### Evacuation Chaos

| Finding | Statistic |
|---------|-----------|
| Knew about accident before evacuation order | Only 20% in host town |
| Received evacuation training | Only 10-15% in nearby areas |
| Were told nuclear accident possible | Less than 10% |
| Evacuated 4+ times in one year | ~70% of nearest residents |
| Evacuated to high radiation areas | ~50% of Namie residents |
| Had no information about radiation levels | Majority until IAEA team arrived |

### Why Did People Evacuate Multiple Times?

The chaos was caused by **constantly changing zones, incomplete information, and bureaucratic failures**:

1. **Zone kept expanding** — 3km → 10km → 20km → "deliberate evacuation area"
2. **Hotspots discovered later** — areas thought safe later found to have high radiation (e.g., Iitate, Kawamata)
3. **No SPEEDI data released** — residents had no way to know which direction radiation was spreading
4. **Slower information transmission** — municipalities 10km+ away learned about accident 12+ hours after notification
5. **Mixed messages** — "no immediate health effects" said repeatedly, but evacuation orders kept expanding

### Evacuation Timeline for Residents

| When | What happened |
|------|---------------|
| March 11, 21:23 | 3km zone ordered — only 20% in Futaba knew about accident |
| March 12, 5:44 | Most residents within 10km finally learn about accident |
| March 12-15 | Zone expands to 10km, then 20km |
| March 15, 11:00 | Shelter-in-place ordered for 20-30km zone |
| March 25 | Voluntary evacuation called for 30km area |
| April 22 | "Deliberate evacuation area" designated northwest of plant |
| Ongoing | Residents moved multiple times based on radiation readings |

### Survivor Accounts of Evacuation Hell

> *"We moved six places inside and outside of the prefecture."* — Namie resident

> *"It took 6 hours by car instead of 1 hour. On my way there, my son who lives away called and told me that I should not expect to return soon."* — Futaba resident

> *"We didn't know there was a hydrogen explosion at the plant, so we couldn't guess why we had to evacuate."* — Minamisoma resident

> *"I could not bring my parents' medical records. My parents' conditions worsened during evacuation. It is hard especially for elderly people to flee unprepared."* — Tomioka resident

### The Death Toll from Poor Evacuation

| Cause | Estimated deaths |
|-------|------------------|
| Direct radiation exposure | ~1 (WHO projected cancer death) |
| **Evacuation-related deaths** | **~2,000** |

The largest group of "disaster victims" died not from radiation, but from:
- Elderly patients dying during transport from hospitals and care homes
- Chronic illness patients (dialysis, chemotherapy) who lost access to treatment
- Stress-induced heart attacks, strokes, diabetes complications
- Suicide and alcoholism from displacement trauma

This is why the NAIIC report states the **evacuation itself became a manmade disaster within the disaster**.

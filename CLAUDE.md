# LANG1407 Case Presentation: Fukushima Nuclear Disaster 2011

## Overview

This folder contains materials for an engineering ethics case presentation on the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear accident (March 2011). The assignment requires a **10-12 minute group presentation** followed by a **6-8 minute seminar discussion**.

**Group size**: Maximum 4 students
**Weight**: 35% of total grade

---

## The Four "Ds" Framework

All presentations must cover these four components:

| D | Focus | Key Questions |
|---|-------|---------------|
| **Describe** | Stakeholders, facts, causes, harm | What happened? Who was involved? What went wrong? |
| **Determine** | Ethical issues, problem definition | What is ethically wrong? Which codes were breached? (HKIE Rules of Conduct, WFEO Model Code) |
| **Discuss** | Options available to stakeholders | What could have been done differently? What alternatives existed? |
| **Decide** | Evaluation and recommendation | Which option is most ethical? Justify your choice |

## Key Case Themes

From the provided materials:

1. **Man-Made Disaster** — The accident was preventable; collusion between TEPCO and regulators
2. **Design Flaws** — 5 reactors had known vulnerabilities that weren't fixed
3. **Warning Signs Ignored** — Multiple warnings about tsunami risks were dismissed
4. **Regulatory Capture** — Nuclear safety regulators were not independent
5. **Emergency Response Failures** — Evacuation delays, communication breakdowns
6. **Leadership Under Crisis** — Masao Yoshida's decisions (ignored orders to cool reactors)
7. **Alarmism vs Actual Risk** — Overreaction may have caused more harm than radiation itself
8. **Systemic Normal Accidents** — Complex systems with catastrophic potential are inherently risky

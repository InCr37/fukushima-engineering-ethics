# DETERMINE — What Was Ethically Wrong?

The Fukushima disaster was not caused by an unforeseeable tsunami. It was a **manmade catastrophe** — the result of ethical failures at multiple levels. Here are three key ethical issues:

---

## 1. Faster, Better, Cheaper vs Safety

-Why no countermeasures is made to prevent tsunami

TEPCO operated under a business logic: new safety regulations would **interfere with operations and reduce profits**.

When experts proposed tsunami countermeasures, TEPCO argued these would be "too expensive." The Federation of Electric Power Companies actively lobbied against stronger regulations (2_inevitability.txt, line 55).

**WFEO principle:** Engineers shall reject practices that harm public welfare.

Conclution: Safety could not be sold

---

## 2. Engineers Must Plan for Failure, Not Just Prevention

-Why when tsunami comes, the plant and the government is unprepared at all.

There is no emergency training. Evacuation is chaotic and communicaiton is delayed. The evac zone is keep changing over a year. Some people are evaced to higher radiation areas and some are being evacuated 4+ time in a year. 

The former head of TEPCO said: *"the risk of a tsunami never crossed his mind"* when he was president (2_inevitability.txt, line 51).

This is **arrogant engineering**. Engineers must assume crisis can hit any second and prepare for it (2_inevitability.txt, lines 79-80). Arrogant to nuclear power

**The resilience question:** What happens when multiple systems fail simultaneously? How do we limit damage? How do we recover quickly?

Conclution: Like a house that prevents fire but also does fire drills — prevention alone is not enough.

---

## 3. Multi-Departmental Monitoring Failed / Collusion

-Why the big environment makes things go wrong as a whole

Multiple organizations were supposed to check each other. Instead, they **colluded**.

| Organization | Role | Problem |
|------------|------|---------|
| METI | Promotes nuclear power | Made NISA part of their ministry |
| NISA | Nuclear regulator | Not independent — captured by METI |
| TEPCO | Plant operator | Resisted regulations, hid risks |

This is called **"regulatory capture"** — regulators serve the industry they should oversee (2_inevitability.txt, line 15).

**HKIE 3.4:** Engineers must advise consequences when their judgment is overruled by non-technical authority. But engineers who warned were overruled, ignored, or punished. The whistleblower at Millstone was fired and blacklisted (2_inevitability.txt, line 33).

---

## The Core Problem

| Ethical Principle | What Should Have Been | What Actually Happened |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Independence | Regulators free from industry | NISA inside METI |
| Accountability | Consequences for violations | Fines < one day's profit |
| Public welfare | Safety over profits | Cost-cutting on safety |

---

## Key Quote

> *"Regulations are only as good as their enforcement... Workers at Fukushima reported that they had advance warnings of inspections, and inspectors regularly winked at violations."*
> — 2_inevitability.txt, line 37

---

## Summary

Fukushima was not an inevitable natural disaster. It was the predictable result of:
1. **Cost over safety** decisions
2. **No failure recovery planning**
3. **Systemic collusion** that silenced warnings

These are not individual errors — they are **systemic ethical failures**.

# The Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Accident
## A Detailed Chronological Account — March 11-15, 2011

---

## Background Context

**The Plant**
Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant (owned by TEPCO) had 6 boiling water reactors:
- Units 1-3: Operating at time of earthquake
- Units 4-6: Under periodical inspection
- Combined power capability: 5,480 MWe
- Protected by a **10m sea wall** (tsunami reached 14m)

**The Morning of March 11, 2011**
At 14:46 local time, a **Magnitude 9.0 earthquake** — the strongest recorded in Japan's history — struck off the coast. It generated a massive tsunami with waves up to **15 meters high**.

---

## Detailed Timeline

### MARCH 11, 2011

#### 14:46 — THE EARTHQUAKE
- The Great East Japan Earthquake strikes 130km east of Sendai, 163km northeast of Fukushima
- Units 1, 2, and 3 are operating normally but immediately trigger **SCRAM** (emergency shutdown)
- seismic tremors damage the electricity transmission line between TEPCO Shinfukushima Transformer Substations and Fukushima Daiichi
- **Loss of off-site AC electricity** occurs

#### 14:47-14:52 — EMERGENCY SYSTEMS ENGAGE
- Emergency diesel generators **automatically activate**
- Unit 1's isolation condensers (IC) automatically activate at 14:52
- Reactor core cooling by isolation condenser begins for Unit 1
- Units 2 and 3 begin core cooling via Reactor Core Isolation Cooling System (RCIC)

#### 14:52-15:03 — IC SHUTDOWN (CRITICAL ERROR)
- **Unit 1 operator manually stops both IC systems** — 11 minutes after automatic activation
- TEPCO claims this was because "reactor coolant temperature excursion rate could not be kept within 55°C benchmark"
- **Worker testimony later revealed**: IC was stopped to check for coolant leaks from IC and other pipes, not because of temperature
- This decision left Unit 1 without automatic cooling much earlier than necessary

#### 15:27-15:37 — TSUNAMI STRIKES
- First tsunami wave detected 1.5km offshore at 15:27
- **Tsunami peak waves reach the plant at approximately 15:38**
- Waves exceed the 10m sea wall, reaching **14 meters height**
- Tsunami floods, damages, and blocks the water intake buildings
- **All emergency diesel generators are destroyed** — flooded and inoperable

#### 15:38 — TOTAL STATION BLACKOUT (SBO)
- Complete loss of all electricity at the plant
- Both external power lines failed (different source stations, but both knocked out by earthquake)
- Backup 66kV transmission line from Tohoku Electric Power Company **fails due to mismatched sockets**
- The **DC battery backup systems are also lost** — not accounted for in SBO planning
- All four reactors (Units 1-4) lose power simultaneously

#### 15:38-17:00 — CHAOS AND LOSS OF CONTROL
- Sudden loss of monitoring equipment: scales, meters, control functions in central control room
- **Lighting and communications knocked out**
- Workers trapped in debris-strewn plant with **manhole covers disappeared, gaping holes in ground**
- After shocks continue to hinder movement
- Decisions must be made **"on the spot by operational staff at the site, absent valid tools and manuals"**
- Portable **radios don't work** inside the reactor buildings due to concrete interference

---

### MARCH 11 — EVENING

#### 16:45 — ARTICLE 15 NOTIFICATION (OFFICIAL REPORT)
- TEPCO files Article 15 notification to the government
- Report is filed but **not effectively communicated** to local governments or residents

#### 18:10 — UNIT 1 REACTOR CORE EXPOSURE BEGINS (ESTIMATED)
- Without cooling, decay heat (approximately 20 MW) begins heating the reactor core
- Water temperature rises rapidly
- Reactor core isolation pump (battery-driven) eventually stops functioning
- **Core exposure begins** — fuel cladding starts ballooning at 1,200K

#### 18:50 — UNIT 1 CORE DAMAGE BEGINS (ESTIMATED)
- Temperature exceeds point where fuel cladding fails
- Partial meltdown of fuel elements begins
- Zirconium-water reaction starts (releasing hydrogen)

#### 19:03 — STATE OF EMERGENCY DECLARED
- Prime Minister's Office (Kantei) declares nuclear emergency
- But **communication chain is broken** — Kantei begins contacting TEPCO headquarters and Fukushima site directly, disrupting planned chain of command
- TEPCO-Kantei response team created at headquarters has **no legal authority**

#### 21:23 — EVACUATION ORDER FOR 3KM ZONE
- Only **20% of residents in Futaba (host town)** know about the accident when this is issued
- Most residents learn about the accident only when evacuation order is issued at 5:44 the next morning
- **12+ hours have passed** since Article 15 notification

#### Late Night March 11 — 21:00+
- Masao Yoshida, plant manager, arrives at the site
- Takes command of desperate recovery efforts
- Workers begin attempting manual cooling procedures with whatever equipment they can find

---

### MARCH 12, 2011

#### 00:30-05:00 — DESPERATE COOLING ATTEMPTS
- Workers attempt to restore cooling using fire trucks and portable pumps
- Multiple attempts fail due to debris, lack of equipment, continued aftershocks
- Unit 2's RCIC continues operating, buying time

#### 05:46 — FRESHWATER INJECTION BEGINS AT UNIT 1
- Workers finally begin injecting fresh water into Unit 1

#### 11:36 — UNIT 2 RCIC SHUTS DOWN
- The Isolation Cooling System for Unit 2 stops
- Battery power exhausted
- **Unit 2 now without cooling**

#### 12:35 — HIGH-PRESSURE COOLANT INJECTION (HPCI) ACTIVATED
- HPCI system activated for Unit 2
- Provides emergency core cooling

#### 14:30 — VENTING ATTEMPTED
- TEPCO attempts to vent pressure from Unit 1 containment to prevent explosion
- Venting procedure is unclear — diagrams incomplete or missing
- Manual confusion about which valves to open

#### 15:36 — **HYDROGEN EXPLOSION AT UNIT 1**
- Hydrogen gas (from zirconium-water reaction) ignites
- **Massive explosion destroys reactor building**
- Debris scattered across the site, hindering rescue operations
- Workers on upper floors of Unit 1 barely escape

#### 16:00 — SUBCONTRACTED WORKERS EVACUATE
- Most subcontracted workers leave the site
- **The Fukushima 50 remain** — approximately 50-100 workers who volunteer to stay and fight the disaster

#### 19:04 — SEAWATER INJECTION BEGINS
- Masao Yoshida orders seawater injection into Unit 1
- This is against TEPCO headquarters orders

---

### MARCH 13, 2011

#### 02:42 — UNIT 2 HPCI SHUTS DOWN
- **Unit 2 now completely without active cooling**

#### 09:10 — UNIT 2 REACTOR CORE EXPOSURE BEGINS

#### 09:20 — VENTING ATTEMPTED AT UNIT 2

#### 09:25 — FRESHWATER INJECTION CONTINUES AT UNIT 1

#### ~10:40 — UNIT 2 CORE DAMAGE BEGINS
- **Backflow of hydrogen from Unit 1 via Standby Gas Treatment System (SGTS)**
- Hydrogen migrates to Unit 2 through shared ventilation systems

#### 11:01 — **HYDROGEN EXPLOSION AT UNIT 3**
- Explosion destroys Unit 3 reactor building
- Multiple workers injured

#### 13:25 — DIAGNOSIS OF UNIT 2 RCIC SHUTDOWN
- TEPCO finally confirms Unit 2 has been without cooling for extended period

---

### MARCH 14, 2011

#### ~06:00 — **SEVERE DAMAGE TO UNIT 2 SUPPRESSION CHAMBER**
- **Hydrogen explosion at Unit 2** — containment vessel itself is damaged
- This is potentially more dangerous than Units 1 and 3 (which exploded externally)
- **Mass discharge of radioactive material**

#### ~17:00 — UNIT 3 REACTOR CORE EXPOSURE

#### ~19:20 — UNIT 3 CORE DAMAGE BEGINS

#### 19:54 — SEAWATER INJECTION BEGINS AT UNIT 3

---

### MARCH 15, 2011

#### 06:00 — **UNIT 4 SPENT FUEL POOL CONCERN**
- Reports of strange sounds at Unit 4
- Concerns that spent fuel in Unit 4 pool may be overheating
- Workers attempt to assess situation but communications and access remain severely compromised

#### 09:00+ — MULTIPLE EVACUATION ZONE EXPANSIONS
- Evacuation zone expanded multiple times
- 20km radius → then 30km
- Some areas receiving no guidance, residents left to self-evacuate or stay

## What Made This Worse: The Chain of Failures

### 1. Pre-Existing Vulnerabilities (YEARS before)
- **Design flaw**: 5 of 6 reactors had older, more vulnerable design
- **No tsunami countermeasures** despite warnings since 2006
- **Antiseismic reinforcements incomplete**: TEPCO postponed deadline from 2009 to January 2016
- **Zero reinforcements** added to Units 1-3 by March 2011
- **Regulatory capture**: NISA part of METI, not independent

### 2. Immediate Pre-Crash Errors (MINUTES after earthquake)
- Unit 1 operator **manually shut off IC** for unclear reasons
- Backup power line failed due to **mismatched sockets**
- No one anticipated **total SBO including DC power loss**

### 3. Crisis Response Failures (HOURS after tsunami)
- **No working communications** inside plant
- **Venting procedures unclear** — diagrams incomplete
- **Government插手** disrupted chain of command
- TEPCO HQ ordered seawater injection stopped (Masao Yoshida disobeyed)
- **Information to public delayed 12+ hours**

### 4. cascading Compound Failures
- Unit 1 hydrogen migrated to Unit 2 through shared systems
- Each unit's failure worsened others
- The "Fukushima 50" were working with **no dosimeters, no consent, no information**

## The Death Toll Reality

| Cause | Estimated Count |
|-------|----------------|
| Direct earthquake/tsunami deaths | ~18,000 |
| Evacuation-related deaths (indirect) | ~2,000 |
| Radiation-related cancer deaths | 1 (projected, WHO 2013) |
| Acute radiation syndrome deaths | 0 |

**The true catastrophe within the catastrophe: approximately 2,000 people died from the stress, displacement, and disrupted medical care of evacuation — not from radiation.**

---

## Key Decision Point: Seawater Injection

On **March 12 at 19:04**, Masao Yoshida ordered seawater injection into Unit 1 despite TEPCO headquarters ordering him to stop. Headquarters was concerned that seawater would corrode the equipment.

Yoshida **disobeyed the order** and continued using seawater to cool the reactor. He later lied to headquarters saying he had stopped, when in fact he continued.

**Why this mattered**: This decision likely prevented complete meltdown and a far worse disaster.

Prime Minister Naoto Kan later publicly defended Yoshida.

## What Survivors Said

> *"I left my house with only the bare necessities. I learned where to go through the emergency radio system while I was on the road. It took 6 hours by car instead of 1 hour."*

> *"If there had been even a word about a nuclear power plant when the evacuation was ordered, we could have reacted reasonably, taken our valuables with us or locked up the house before we had left. We had to run with nothing but the clothes we were wearing."*

> *"We didn't know there was a hydrogen explosion at the plant, so we couldn't guess why we had to evacuate. That sort of information should have been announced to the nearby residents instantly."*

---

## Summary

The Fukushima disaster was not a story of one catastrophic failure — it was a **cascading collapse** of:
1. Known vulnerabilities that were never fixed
2. Multiple co-occurring failures that overwhelmed emergency systems
3. Broken communication chains that prevented effective response
4. Organizational failures that put production over safety

**The earthquake and tsunami were the trigger. The disaster was baked in years earlier.**

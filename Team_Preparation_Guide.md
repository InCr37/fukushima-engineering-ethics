- **Group Size**: Maximum 4 students

- **Group Components**: Task Achievement (10%), Interaction (5%)

---

## The Four "Ds" Framework

Your presentation must include:

1. **Describe** — Stakeholders, facts of the case, causes, and harm caused
2. **Determine** — Ethical issues, how to define the problem, what is wrong, ethical codes breached
3. **Discuss** — Options available to stakeholders (the subject chose one action, but what were the alternatives?)
4. **Decide** — Which option is most ethical (evaluate all options and select the best)

---

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. Task Achievement (Group - 10%)

**What markers look for:**

- All task aspects fully addressed with a clear, audience-appropriate response
- Ideas and reasoning fully and consistently developed
- Clear, consistent stance or purpose throughout
- Full conformance to genre conventions

**Tips:**
- Address all four "Ds" completely
- Present information in a logical sequence
- Have a clear thesis/stance that runs through the entire presentation
- Focus mainly on analysis, not just facts

### 2. Audience Engagement (Individual - 5%)

**What markers look for:**

- Audience interest sustained throughout
- Persuasion achieved through compelling opening, logical content, impactful closing
- Adaptive communication tailored to audience needs
- Strategies employed to foster connection and responsiveness

**Tips:**
- Open compellingly — hook your audience in the first 30 seconds
- Close impactfully — leave a lasting impression
- Adjust your tone, style, and approach based on audience response
- Make sure the audience understands why the topic matters to them

### 3. Interaction (Group - 5%)

**What markers look for:**

- Discussion managed professionally with strong rapport
- Insightful questions posed, responses delivered in depth
- Communication adapted to promote exchanges, maintaining clarity and attentiveness
- Active participation from all group members

**Tips:**
- Ask relevant, thought-provoking questions during the seminar
- Build on others' ideas — don't just respond, expand
- Take turns naturally, clarify when needed
- Show genuine interest in audience perspectives

### 4. Delivery (Individual - 15%)

**Verbal:**
- Natural, fluent delivery without awkward pauses
- Effective use of pace, volume, rhythm, intonation
- Clear pronunciation

**Non-verbal:**
- Professional body language (eye contact, gestures, posture, facial expressions)
- Purposeful non-verbal cues that show enthusiasm

**Visuals:**
- Attractive, relevant, and properly labeled graphics
- Visual aids complement and enhance the message
- Consistent style across all slides

**Tips:**
- Practice until you can present without notes
- Do NOT rely on notes during the presentation
- Rehearse with timing to ensure you fit within 10-12 minutes
- Every group member should speak for approximately the same length of time

## Seminar Discussion Preparation

### Question Strategy

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Opening** | Warm up audience, connect to case | "What initial thoughts do you have about this situation?" |
| **Probing** | Dig deeper into ethical issues | "What would you say to someone who argues that X was justified because Y?" |
| **Alternative** | Explore different perspectives | "If you were in the stakeholder's position, what would you have done differently?" |
| **Ethical Code** | Connect to professional standards | "How does this align with the HKIE Rules of Conduct?" |
| **Consequence** | Examine impact of decisions | "What could have happened if the subject chose option B instead?" |
| **Reflection** | Encourage critical thinking | "What lessons can engineers learn from this case?" |

### During the Seminar

- Listen actively to audience responses
- Build on answers — don't just move to next question
- Keep discussion focused and natural
- Manage time (6-8 minutes)
- Show genuine interest in different viewpoints
